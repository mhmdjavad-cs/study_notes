Two models $\mathcal{M}$ and $\mathcal{N}$ of the same language $\mathcal{L}$ are **elementarily equivalent** if:

They satisfy exactly the same first-order sentences in the language $\mathcal{L}$.

Formally, this means:

$$
\mathcal{M} \equiv \mathcal{N} \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \text{for every first-order sentence } \varphi \text{ in } \mathcal{L}, \quad
\mathcal{M} \models \varphi \iff \mathcal{N} \models \varphi.
$$
